German Shepard

The German Shepherd (German: Deutscher Schäferhund, German pronunciation: [ˈʃɛːfɐˌhʊnt]) is a breed of medium to large-sized working dog that originated in Germany. The breed's officially recognized name is German Shepherd Dog in the English language (sometimes abbreviated as GSD). The breed was once known as the Alsatian in Britain and Ireland

  • Other names Alsatian wolf dog Berger Allemand Deutscher Schäferhund Schäferhund
  • Origin Germany
  • Weight Male 30–40 kg (66–88 lb) Female 22–32 kg (49–71 lb)
  • Height Male 60–65 cm (24–26 in) Female 55–60 cm (22–24 in)
  • Colour Most commonly tan with black saddlery
  • Litter size 4-9
  • Life span 9-13 years
  • Personality Smart, confident, courageous, and steady; a true dog lover's dog
